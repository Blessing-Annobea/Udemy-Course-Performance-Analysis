## UDEMY COURSE PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS
This project centres on examining popularity, engagement levels, and pricing insights across four primary subjects: Business Finance, Web Development, Musical Instruments, and Graphic Design on a well-known online learning platform called UDEMY from 2011 to 2017.


## Dataset Overview
•	Four subject areas:
Business Finance, Graphic Design, Musical Instruments & Web Development.

•	Seven Variables Analyzed:
Subject, Level, Price, Subscribers, Reviews, Lectures, Publication Date.


## Data Cleaning and Preparation
•	Removed duplicated records where necessary and checked for missing values in all key variables.

•	Standardized numeric and date formats to ensure consistency.

•	Created Payment Type (Free vs Paid) and Price Category fields.

•	Created Revenue metric by multiplying Price by Number of Subscribers.


## Tools Used
•	Excel

•	Power Query


## Project Objectives
•	Identify the most popular course subjects and levels.

•	Evaluate the relationship between reviews and subscriber numbers.

•	Assess how pricing influences course enrollment.

•	Provide actionable recommendations to improve course performance.

## Key Insights
•	Web Development accounts for approximately 68% of total subscribers; the most popular subject by a substantial margin.

•	All Levels courses attract the highest number of subscribers across the platform.

•	Courses with higher review counts consistently attract more subscribers across all subject areas.

•	Web Development has the largest and most diversified course catalogue across all levels.

•	Free courses attract significantly more subscribers than paid courses across all subject categories

•	Web Development generates the highest revenue among all subject areas on the platform.


## Proposed Recommendations
•	The Web Development catalogue should be expanded and specialized topics such as cloud computing and AI, as well as bundle related courses should be introduced into career pathways.

•	Instructors should structure courses to begin with foundational concepts and progressively introduce advanced material for both beginners and experienced learners.

•	Course creators should actively encourage reviews through in-course prompts, post-completion reminders and by maintaining high-quality content.

•	Expand course offerings in underrepresented subjects and create balanced content across all levels to capture unmet learner demand.

•	Use free or low-cost introductory courses to build audience reach and trust, then convert learners into higher-priced advanced courses.

•	Invest in premium Web Development courses, certification prep content and advanced technical topics to capitalize on strong learner willingness to pay.


## Remarks
By optimizing pricing, encouraging reviews and expanding high-demand content, creators can significantly increase enrollment and profitability.
